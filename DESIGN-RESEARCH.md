# Doghouse Productions — Design Research & Additions Menu

Synthesis of 5 parallel research passes (2026-06-15): Kairosoft/Game Dev Story,
the PC descendants (Game Dev Tycoon / Mad Games Tycoon 2 / Software Inc), broader
tycoon best-sellers (Two Point, RimWorld, Tropico, Stardew, Planet Zoo, Prison
Architect), management-sim design theory (Cook, Meier, Koster, Juice It or Lose It),
and Steam commercial-success practice (GameDiscoverCo, presskit.gg, Next Fest data).

Each addition is mapped to what the game **already has** so nothing here is a dupe.
Already built: genre×vibe synergy grid (S/A/B/C), 4-critic /40, crew traits, hire/train,
two-loop economy (creative→buzz→events→members→MRR vs $8k burn), per-era trends,
choice-driven random events, story beats, buildable spaces, polish/spark, legendary hall.

Impact/Effort scale: ▲▲▲ high … ▲ low · effort S/M/L.

---

## Tier A — Core-loop depth (makes each project a real decision)

1. **Per-project Focus + phase sliders** — before production, pick a posture
   (Quality / Fast / Experimental) and split effort across Concept / Build / Promo
   phases against a hidden genre-ideal. Mastery = hitting the ideal without a guide.
   *GDS direction modes + GDT/MGT2 sliders. Most-praised depth mechanic in the genre.*
   Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort M
2. **Mid-project risk spike** — at ~40% a one-time gamble: push for a breakthrough
   (+big points) vs risk a spiral (+rough edges, −buzz). Tension in the wait loop.
   *GDS "Boost". Cheap drama.* Impact ▲▲ · Effort S
3. **Sequels / franchise catalog** — legendary projects can spawn sequels that inherit
   buzz and pay small **recurring residual revenue** (a back-catalog that compounds).
   Catalog becomes an asset class. *GDS Hall of Fame chain + MGT2 IP system.*
   Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort M
4. **Market research / trend preview** — pay to reveal next era's hot Medium×Vibe a
   month early; reward proactive spend over reactive guessing. *MGT2.* Impact ▲▲ · Effort S
5. **No-dominant-strategy hires** — every trait gets a visible strength AND a hidden
   cost (Ambient Host already does this; extend to all). No hire strictly best.
   *Meier "interesting decisions" + Software Inc.* Impact ▲▲ · Effort S

## Tier B — Retention & emotional attachment (the "I care" layer)

6. **Named regulars + crew bonds** — members become named recurring faces; crew form
   bonds/rivalries that buff or tank shared projects; emergent micro-drama you'd retell.
   *RimWorld/Tropico/Stardew/Software Inc trait-collision — the genre's stickiest hook.*
   Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort L
7. **Annual LA Scene Awards** — every December, your year's work competes vs rival
   studios; nominations + a ceremony = a calendar climax even in a down year.
   *GDS Global Game Awards + Tropico social comparison.* Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort M
8. **Always-on dual goals** — a short-term objective + a long-term unlock always
   visible, so there's never a "nothing to do" trough. *Two Point star-goals.*
   Impact ▲▲ · Effort M
9. **Seasonal modifiers** — calendar seasons shift which events/vibes pop (summer
   rooftop season, winter intimate sets) — novelty without wiping progress.
   *Stardew seasons; fits a real event studio.* Impact ▲▲ · Effort M
10. **Prestige / Legacy reboot (New Game+)** — at a milestone, optionally reset cash+gear
    for a permanent studio trait (e.g. "Cult Following +20% member loyalty"). Faster
    next run. *Meta-progression theory; the #1 fix for "no replay value".* Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort M

## Tier C — Juice & charm (cheap, high-feel)

11. **Per-week production juice** — floating "+N" on axis bars, crew emote/pulse while
    working, streaming-counter tick on release. *Juice It or Lose It.* Impact ▲▲ · Effort S
12. **In-world narrator / Scene Report ticker** — a wry voice reacting to your moves
    (releases, flops, drama). Personality with no UI bloat. *Theme Hospital/Tropico DJ.*
    Impact ▲▲ · Effort S
13. **Absurdist humor pass** — event/flavor copy leans funny so failure reads as content,
    not punishment. Lowers churn on hard sessions. *Two Point/Theme Hospital.* Impact ▲ · Effort S
14. **Variable surprise rewards** — ~30% of releases drop a bonus (rare collab, surprise
    sponsor, viral spike). Variable reinforcement > fixed. Impact ▲▲ · Effort S

## Tier D — Onboarding & transparency (fixes the #1 negative-review driver)

15. **Surface the math** — show expected score band, synergy reason, why a crew fits.
    GDS's most-criticized flaw is hiding this; transparency lets players optimize on
    purpose. *Counter-steal — GDS/MGT2 opacity complaints.* Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort S
16. **Teach via constraint** — first 1–2 projects locked to a guided path; unlock systems
    only as the player clears them. Best onboarding = no tutorial wall. *Koster/Stardew.*
    Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort M

## Tier E — Bigger bets (later / optional)

17. **Rival LA studios (competitor AI)** — named rivals release, chase the same members/
    awards, give the world life. *GDT/Software Inc.* Impact ▲▲▲ · Effort L
18. **Studio layout adjacency** — space placement gives small buffs (recording next to
    lounge, etc.). *Software Inc — but risks micromanagement; keep light.* Impact ▲ · Effort L

## Tier F — Commercial / Steam (roadmap, not code-now)

- **10k wishlists before launch** — below this you're invisible in "Popular Upcoming" (gamediscover.co).
- **Steam Next Fest demo**, released ~3 wks early, ending on a cliffhanger; 68–88% of wishlist
  gains come from the store page, not demo play (presskit.gg).
- **Randomized run seeds + scenario modes** — #1 differentiator between 94%- and 70%-positive
  tycoons; kills "you already know what happens" (steamcommunity.com).
- **Explicit late-game escalation** — "nothing to do after year X" is the top negative review.
- **Steam Workshop data layer** — expose studio/crew/project templates so players make packs.
- **Public EA roadmap**, dev updates ≥ every 30 days (silence reads as "abandoned").
- **Pricing** $9.99 EA → $12.99–14.99 at 1.0; **high-contrast pixel capsule**, one focal point,
  readable at 231×87px, zero marketing text (steampageanalyzer.com).
